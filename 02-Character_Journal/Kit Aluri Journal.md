## Session 05 — Silvered

I don't remember what woke me first — the sound or the smell. Something animal and wrong, the kind of wrongness that hits your body before your brain catches up. One moment I was in the thin, black sleep of the Underdark, and the next I was on my feet with adrenaline searing through my blood and two of our own — Topsy and Turvy — changed into something feral and coming for us.

The fight was a blur. Binks saw them first. By the time the rest of us shook off sleep, they were already on us. Topsy came at me — teeth, claws, the works. She bit me. I felt it go deep, felt the hot, wet sting of it, and for a horrible second I thought: this is how it starts. This is how you become one of them.

I don't know what I grabbed first. My hand closed around the crowbar in my pack, and I swung. And I kept swinging until Topsy stopped moving and the shape on the floor wasn't a wererat anymore, just a small gnome, crumpled and still.

After — when the shaking stopped — I checked myself. The bite was already closing. Not right, not clean, but fast. I know what I am. I'm an orc. My blood runs hot and hard, and I've shrugged off things that would put a human flat. But this was different. The sting faded too fast, and for a few breaths I stood there testing the edges of my own body like it might have been replaced with something else in the dark.

Then I looked at the crowbar. The tip was silver. Not just silver-colored — actual silver, bright and hard, with Topsy's blood sizzling where it touched the metal. I picked this crowbar up in Velkynvelve. I've held it in my hands every day since. I know what it is. What it was.

It was not silver before.

The others noticed. Amanita waved her hands over it — transmutation magic, she said. Something changed the metal. I could have told her that. What I wanted to know was *who*. Or what. Nobody had an answer.

We argued about Turvy for what felt like hours. He was unconscious, tied to a chair, barely alive. I wanted him dead. I said it plainly. He's a wererat. His sister just tried to kill us. The Underdark doesn't give second chances, and neither do I. Aeolus agreed. Darby, predictably, went soft — wouldn't hear of killing a bound creature. Amanita wanted to talk to him. Binks started going on about Blingdenstone and wererats and complicated political history like we were at a summit and not standing over a corpse.

Binks healed him. Turvy woke up and saw his sister's body — we'd covered her with a blanket, but her arm was sticking out, and he knew immediately. He crumpled. Just — folded in on himself and started keening. I've heard death sounds before. I've made them. This was a small gnome who'd lost the only family he had left, and he was making a sound like something tearing loose inside him.

He said the light changed. The faerzress — the weird magical glow that suffuses everything down here — turned dark, and then he doesn't remember anything. Says they had a substance, some kind of jam, that helped them control the change. It didn't work here. Something in this place broke whatever mechanism they used to stay in control.

I crouched down next to him. Put my hand near his throat. Told him: "I killed your sister because she tried to kill me. I don't want to hear it. I'm a fucking orc." He looked at me like I was the worst thing he'd ever seen. He might be right.

I tried to knock him out after that. Binks asked me to. I swung the crowbar — missed. Tried again — connected, put him down to almost nothing. Then tried with my fist and missed entirely. That's when Blarg stepped in and took over, speaking to Turvy quietly about family and loss and the kinds of things I don't have words for.

Blarg found something in the supplies — a flask with a gemstone that pulses with moonlight. Some kind of lunar enchantment. He gave it to Turvy along with healing. Told him it might help him control the changes. Turvy held it like a drowning man holds driftwood.

We finally rested. The first real sleep since Velkynvelve. I asked Aeolus to take watch with Amanita. I do not trust easily, but I trust Aeolus to stay awake and I trust Amanita to notice things. Turvy cried himself to sleep. Blarg spoke to him first — I don't know what he said. When I closed my eyes, the dark was just dark, and nothing in it moved.

When we woke, Binks started poking at a table that had been bothering him since we arrived — it was positioned wrong, like it had been moved. He slid it aside and the others started investigating the floor. Amanita found something — a network of fungal threads running beneath the stone, connected to something deeper. Stool confirmed: mushrooms below, and Neverlight Grove is somewhere out there but far.

Amanita took a mithral pickaxe from the supplies and struck the floor. The stone peeled back like a scab. Spores erupted. The floor opened into a cavity about ten feet down, filled with mycelium. Three tunnels branched off below: one north, small and crawlable; one down a stone ladder to the west; and a deeper passage that angled down like a slide.

Binks cast some kind of ritual — his trickster god answering questions about which path to take. The slide: bad, but interesting. The dwarven ladder: safe, boring. He chose the slide. Of course he did.

The floor gave way further. Binks went down. The rest of us scrambled back from the edge. He's somewhere below us now, in the dark, and we're standing above a hole in the ground trying to figure out whether to follow the gnome who just dove headfirst into the worst possible option because his god thought it would be funny.

I held to that and I do not regret it. The tunnels below are elf-sized so I hung back. Yanked Amanita out when the floor started going. That part I can do.
