## Kit Aluri — POV Journal

### THE THINGS WE CARRIED IN THE DARK: A SOLDIER'S MEMOIR

We hit the water hard.

The pool was deeper than it looked — deeper than anyone expected — and for the first few seconds there was nothing but cold black water and the muffled sounds of chaos above. The vrocks were still screaming. The webbing was still burning. The drow were still fighting for their lives up on those platforms, and we were sinking like stones with our packs and our stolen gear dragging us down.

I found my footing first. Orc legs, orc lungs — I was already moving before the shock wore off. The water was chest-deep on me, which meant it was neck-deep on Binks. I saw him go under and I grabbed him before I even thought about it. Hauled him up by whatever I could reach — collar, pack strap, I don't remember — and held him there while the others surfaced around us. His face was white. He'd rolled badly coming in. But he kicked hard and found the surface, and for one impossible second he rolled a natural twenty on his athletics and swam like he'd been born to it. The gnome can surprise you.

Darby didn't surface. She'd gone straight to the bottom — deliberately — and come back up dragging two full chests of equipment through the muck like they weighed nothing. Athletics check: natural twenty plus six. The Goliath just walked along the bottom of the pool carrying everything. Meanwhile Amanita was trying to dive and kept bobbing back up like a cork. Aeolus went under trying to help and nearly drowned himself.

Then Darby hit something. She slipped forward, hands down in this gray slick on the shallows, and the voice hit all of us at once — inside our heads, not through our ears. "Flesh for the Faceless Lord." A gray ooze, bubbled up out of the shallows between us and dry ground, and suddenly we had a fight on our hands while half the party was still treading water.

Binks got clear first. Cast some kind of divine healing on Aeolus and shouted at us to keep running. Amanita reached into the thing with bare hands and hit it with chill touch — dark necrotic energy — then coughed up a cloud of spores she didn't even know she had. "Oh my god, I can produce spores. I'm a mushroom." Seven damage. In the middle of a fight for our lives, the elf discovered she's part fungus and was delighted about it.

The ooze critted Amanita. Fourteen damage — bludgeoning and acid. She went down hard. I was already moving. Grabbed Darby and dragged her clear of the thing, used my Adrenaline Rush to get us twenty feet away. Told everyone to stop touching it and get moving. Darby doubled back anyway — whipped the thing for nine damage because Darby doesn't listen when she's angry.

Blarg saved Amanita. Swam to her, cast Cure Wounds at second level — twenty-three hit points. I've never seen a heal that big. The dwarf patched her up from near death to fighting shape in six seconds flat, then hauled them both to shore.

Blarg finished the ooze with Guiding Bolt. Thirteen radiant damage. The thing melted back into the pool and didn't come back.

After that, we ran. Two hours, fast pace, north through the tunnels. No one followed. The adrenaline burned off somewhere in the first hour and by the time we stopped for a short rest, every one of us had a point of exhaustion. We inventoried gear, argued about routes, and tried to figure out where the hell we were going.

Binks knows these tunnels — or at least the general direction. Blingdenstone, the deep gnome city, is north. Two weeks, maybe more. The kuo-toa town of Sloobludop is closer — ten days — but off our current path. The duergar city of Gracklstugh is south. Topsy and Turvy seemed reluctant to go back to Blingdenstone, which Binks noticed but couldn't explain.

We went north. Toward Blinks's home, toward the drow capital of Menzoberranzan (which is a hell of a trade-off), and away from everything else.

The tunnels opened into a mushroom cavern. Fire beetles — a dozen of them, glowing orange-red, munching on toadstool caps that Amanita identified as Timmask, the devil's mushroom. Poisonous spores if disturbed. Binks cast Pass Without Trace and we crept through with a plus ten to stealth. Even Darby made it, though Binks slapped her with some blessing first that gave her advantage on the roll.

On the far side, Aeolus spotted something the rest of us missed. His eyes see further than anyone's — a hundred and twenty feet, through magical darkness, through everything. Devil's Sight, he called it. He pointed at a section of wall that didn't match the surrounding stone. Five feet high, five feet wide. Engravings in a language none of us could read.

None of us except Eldeth. The dwarf walked up to it, spoke a word in Dwarvish, and the door dropped open. A short passage leading into what looked like a room.

Blarg — our dwarf — doesn't speak Dwarvish. "I haven't been raised by my family," he said, and left it at that. There's a story there. I didn't ask.

We're standing at the threshold of something. Could be shelter. Could be a tomb. Either way, it's better than the open tunnel with drow behind us and the dark ahead.

## Session Analysis

### Narrative Summary

Session 03 opens in the immediate aftermath of the S02 escape. The party lands in the pool beneath [[04-World-Lore/Locations/Velkynvelve]], still under fire from above. The drow are occupied fighting the vrocks and chasme, buying the party time to recover gear and get to dry land. [[Darby Stonefeather]] dives for equipment chests (athletics nat 20+6) while [[Binks Stonevein]] nearly drowns (athletics nat 1+3) before being rescued by [[Kit Aluri]] (athletics 17). [[Aeolus]] also struggles in the water (athletics 3).

A [[Gray Ooze]] emerges in the shallows, telepathically broadcasting "Flesh for the Faceless Lord" — the campaign's first reference to Juiblex, the demon lord of oozes. Combat is brief but significant: [[Amanita de'Champignon]] discovers her Halo of Spores ability mid-fight, [[Blarg]] delivers a massive 23 HP Cure Wounds (2nd level) to save Amanita from near death, and Blarg finishes the ooze with Guiding Bolt (13 radiant). [[Darby Stonefeather]] attacks with her whip for 9 damage. Kit drags Darby clear of the ooze and uses Adrenaline Rush to get distance.

After combat, the party runs north at fast pace for approximately two hours, earning one level of exhaustion each. During a short rest, they inventory gear, distribute rations (30 days between Binks and Amanita), and discuss route options. Binks provides knowledge of [[04-World-Lore/Locations/Blingdenstone]] (deep gnome city, ~2 weeks north). Amanita senses [[04-World-Lore/Locations/Neverlight Grove]] to the south/west via her fungal connection with [[Stool]]. [[Sloobludop]] (kuo-toa town, ~10 days) is identified as a closer but off-path option. The party chooses north toward Blingdenstone.

The session's final act takes place in a mushroom cavern filled with Timmask (devil's mushroom) and fire beetles. Binks casts Pass Without Trace (+10 stealth), and the party sneaks through successfully. On the far side, Aeolus discovers a hidden door with a nat 20 perception check, revealing his Devil's Sight (120ft darkvision piercing magical darkness). [[Eldeth]] opens the dwarven door by speaking a Dwarvish safe word. The session ends at the threshold. Notably, [[Blarg]] — a dwarf — does not speak Dwarvish ("I haven't been raised by my family").

### Themes & Emotional Beats

- **First Steps in Freedom:** The party's first real choices as free people — where to go, how to travel, who to trust. The weight of escape gives way to the weight of survival.
- **The Faceless Lord:** First Juiblex reference. The gray ooze's telepathic message introduces a demon lord presence beyond the vrocks and chasmes. The Underdark is not just dangerous — it's corrupted.
- **Amanita's Awakening:** Discovering Halo of Spores mid-combat. "Oh my god, I can produce spores. I'm a mushroom." A character finding joy in their own nature, even in crisis.
- **Blarg's Compassion:** The 23 HP Cure Wounds on Amanita is the most impactful heal of the campaign so far. Blarg quietly keeps people alive while everyone else fights.
- **Aeolus's Eyes:** Devil's Sight discovery. 120ft darkvision piercing magical darkness. In a campaign set entirely underground, this is a game-changing revelation.
- **Blarg's Displacement:** A dwarf who doesn't speak Dwarvish. "I haven't been raised by my family." A single line that opens a backstory wound.
- **Topsy & Turvy's Reluctance:** Both twins reluctant to return to Blingdenstone. Binks noticed but couldn't determine why. Foreshadowing for S04.
