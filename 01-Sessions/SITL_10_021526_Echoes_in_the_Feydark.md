## Session Analysis

### Narrative Summary

Session 10 marks the party's transition from the Underdark into the [[04-World-Lore/Locations/The Feydark|Feydark]], a fae-touched subterranean realm belonging to the Archfey [[Psilofyr]]. The session opens at the hidden alcove from the end of Session 09, where the party faces a flickering fungal portal and a pursuit level of 4.

After a chaotic sequence involving a spooked [[Roxanne]] (failed animal handling checks from [[Kit Aluri]] and [[Blarg]]), [[Blarg]] stabilizing the portal with an arcana roll of 23 (25 pre-exhaustion), and [[Aeolus]] making a dramatic mid-charge acrobatics leap onto Roxanne (17), the party commits to the portal. [[Amanita de'Champignon]] saves the situation by stepping in front of the charging rothe with food (animal handling 17). [[Kit Aluri]] senses something behind them — a large insectoid creature with tentacles emerges from the pool but narrowly misses her (nat 1 attack) — and the party passes through.

On the other side, thick fog separates the party into three groups: [[Binks Stonevein]] alone, [[Aeolus]]/[[Blarg]]/[[Amanita de'Champignon]] on Roxanne, and [[Kit Aluri]]/[[Darby Stonefeather]] together. Each group except Kit's experiences a personalized vision:

- **Binks** sees his deceased grandfather and two brothers around a table. They share stories, warn of a great evil threatening the Underdark tied to the myconids, and empower his loot pick with a magical glow.
- **Darby** sees her father at an axe beak farm. He delivers a similar warning about a chaotic entity threatening life and death, and empowers her carved trinket. Clementine (spider) grows slightly when the father gives it a scritch.
- **Aeolus/Blarg/Amanita** are guided by Roxanne (who instinctively recognizes the path) and encounter Echo, Aeolus's patron raven, who explains they are in the Feydark — the realm of [[Psilofyr]] (the Carrion King, Archfey of Myconids), near the Court of Stars. Echo warns something is wrong in their future before departing due to fae rules.
- **Kit** experiences no vision. She closes her eyes, draws her greatsword, and enters a defensive warrior meditation. She hears running water, rustling leaves, and wet gliding sounds. No comfort, no guidance, no empowered trinket.

The visions fade, the party reconvenes on a cliffside clearing overlooking a massive gorge, and combat begins immediately against a Myconid Sovereign and several Myconid Adults. DM grants a short rest from the time dilation. [[Blarg]] wild-shapes into a brown bear, earns a critical hit (nat 20, 18 damage), and is rewarded with bonus movement to sprint to the Sovereign. [[Aeolus]] uses Witch Bolt (3d12, 19 damage). [[Stool]] and [[Amanita de'Champignon]] use rapport spores to convince Myconid C to stand down (persuasion 16). [[Kit Aluri]] kills the Sovereign with a Trip Attack leaping off bear-Blarg's back, landing in a superhero pose. [[Roxanne]] charges and gores Myconid D. Four fae goblins arrive mid-combat as allies but the party finishes the fight before they can engage.

After the Sovereign falls, it reconstitutes into a larger humanoid form. [[Psilofyr]] speaks telepathically: "You all have shown your potential. Well done, friend. We will speak soon. But now you must rest." A spore explosion knocks the entire party unconscious. Forced long rest. All PCs level to 4. Session ends with the goblins catching Blarg as he reverts from bear form and laying him down gently.

### Scene / Timeline Breakdown

- **Scene 2: Chaos at the Portal.** Blarg stabilizes the portal (arcana 25-2=23). Aeolus leaps onto Roxanne (acrobatics 17). Amanita stops the charge with food (animal handling 17). Binks is oblivious (perception 2+4-2=4). Marching order established.
- **Scene 3: The Claw.** Kit's passive perception 13 isn't enough to beat the ambush. A large insectoid creature with tentacles reaches from the pool — nat 1 attack, misses. Kit screams "Go now!" and the party rushes through the portal.
- **Scene 4: Arrival in the Feydark.** Thick fog. Party separated into three groups. Constitution saves made (no one affected). Perception checks determine visibility: Amanita (20) and Darby (19) lightly obscured; others heavily obscured/blinded.
- **Scene 5: Binks's Vision.** Binks hears laughter, goes stealth (18 with Guidance), finds his grandfather and two brothers at a table. Religion check (12) reveals divine connection is blocked — not on the material plane. Grandfather warns of coming evil, hints at the Feydark's nature. Binks's loot pick glows with new power.
- **Scene 6: Darby's Vision.** Darby hears her father's axe beak call and sprints into the fog. Finds her dad at a farm. Wisdom check (14) reveals it feels dreamlike but increasingly normal. Father warns of a great evil, empowers her carved trinket with a magical glow. Clementine (spider) grows slightly. The illusion fades — "father" is a myconid.
- **Scene 7: Aeolus/Blarg/Amanita on Roxanne.** Roxanne follows a path she instinctively recognizes. Aeolus hears a crow call. Blarg hears goblin voices. Amanita senses spores guiding her. Lesser Restoration cures Aeolus's blindness. Echo (the raven) lands and explains: they are in the Feydark, realm of [[Psilofyr]] (the Carrion King, Archfey of Myconids). Echo warns something is coming before departing due to fae rules.
- **Scene 8: Kit's Warrior Meditation.** Kit closes her eyes, draws her greatsword, enters a defensive stance. Perception (11-2=9) picks up running water, rustling leaves, and wet gliding sounds. No vision. No danger sense. She waits.
- **Scene 9: The Party Reconvenes.** Visions end. Myconid illusion-figures dissolve into the earth. Fog clears. Party finds themselves on a cliffside clearing. Initiative rolled.
- **Scene 10: Trial of the Myconid Sovereign.** Full combat encounter. ~2 rounds. Blarg wild shapes into brown bear, crits on Myconid E (nat 20, 18 damage), earns bonus movement, sprints to the Sovereign. Aeolus uses Witch Bolt (3d12, 19 damage). Stool's persuasion (16) convinces Myconid C to stand down. Kit kills the Sovereign with a Trip Attack leaping off bear-Blarg. Roxanne charges and gores Myconid D. Four fae goblins arrive as allies.
- **Scene 11: Psilofyr Speaks.** The Sovereign's corpse reconstitutes into a larger humanoid form. Telepathic voice: "You have shown your potential. Well done, friend. We will speak soon. But now you must rest." Spore explosion knocks entire party unconscious.
- **Scene 12: Level Up and Rest.** Forced long rest. All PCs level to 4. Binks takes Rogue (multiclass). Aeolus stays Warlock. Blarg receives moonlight glow as he reverts from bear form; goblins catch and lay him down gently. Binks picks Sylvan. Kit picks Goblin. Session ends.

### Themes & Emotional Beats

- **Trust and Surrender:** The entire session hinges on the party choosing to trust a flickering portal into the unknown. Kit is the only one who doesn't experience a comforting vision — she stays vigilant, eyes closed, sword drawn.
- **Family and Loss:** Binks sees his dead grandfather and brothers. Darby sees her father. Both are given comfort and warnings. The Feydark uses the language of family to communicate with the party.
- **Kit's Isolation:** Kit receives no vision, no comfort, no empowered trinket. She enters warrior meditation alone while everyone else is guided. This is the first time Kit has been narratively singled out by omission.
- **Fae Bargains:** Psilofyr grants gifts (long rest, level-up, trinket empowerment) but also states "We will speak soon" — implying a debt or future obligation. Classic fae structure.
- **Roxanne's Instinct:** The resurrected rothe — amnesiac, confused, and skittish — instinctively recognizes the Feydark path. Her past life may be connected to this place.
- **DM's Homebrew Pride:** Addison explicitly stated this Feydark arc is his own creation ("The actual resource does not go into the Feydark at all"). This is a significant departure from the Out of the Abyss module.

### Encounter Summary

The Myconid Sovereign trial was a structured test by [[Psilofyr]], the Archfey of Myconids. Five Myconid Adults (A-E) and one Myconid Sovereign engaged the party in a cliffside clearing. The party received a short rest benefit from Feydark time dilation before combat.

Key moments: Blarg wild-shaped into a brown bear, rolled a nat 20 claw attack on Myconid E (18 damage), and was rewarded with 15ft bonus movement to reach the Sovereign. Aeolus hit the Sovereign with Witch Bolt for 19 damage (3d12 at 2nd level). Stool used rapport spores with Amanita's help to persuade Myconid C to stand down (persuasion 16). Kit killed the Sovereign with a greatsword strike + Trip Attack superiority die, leaping off bear-Blarg's back and landing in a superhero pose. Roxanne charged and gored Myconid D for 7 piercing damage. Blarg (bear) finished the last myconid with a claw + bite combo (11 + 9 damage).

Myconid Adults had AC 12, used bludgeoning fist attacks + poison damage. Sovereign used multi-attack (fist + Pacifying Spores, Con save to resist sleep). The dying Sovereign attempted a telepathic charm on Amanita ("Creature of the many, help us") — she saved (Wis 17, elf advantage vs. charm).

After the fight, the Sovereign's corpse reconstituted into a larger, more humanoid form. Psilofyr spoke to the party and forced them unconscious with a spore explosion (no save — Archfey override).

## Archivist Notes

### Patterns, Progress & Future Implications

- **Campaign-Level Foreshadowing:** Both Binks's and Darby's visions independently warned of a "great evil" / "chaotic entity" threatening the balance of life and death, connected to the mycelial network spanning the Underdark. This aligns with Out of the Abyss's demon lord incursion arc but is being delivered through Addison's homebrew Feydark expansion. The language about "two beings, both kin and yet not kin" (from Binks's grandfather) may reference Zuggtmoy and Juiblex or a homebrew equivalent.
- **Psilofyr as Quest Giver:** The Archfey explicitly said "we will speak soon," establishing a future obligation. The party passed a combat trial and was granted a long rest + level-up. This follows classic fae bargain structure — gifts given, debts implied.
